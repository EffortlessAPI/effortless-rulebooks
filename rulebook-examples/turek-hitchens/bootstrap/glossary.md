# Glossary — *Does God Exist?* (Hitchens vs. Turek, VCU)

Formal definitions of the debate's vocabulary, grouped by area. Each term gets 1–2 sentences situating its meaning *in the context of this debate*. Re-derived from the full VCU transcript (`bootstrap/raw-input.txt`).

## Worldviews & Positions

- **Theism** — Belief in a personal God who not only created the universe but intervenes in it, answers prayers, and grounds morality. Turek defends this.
- **Deism** — Belief that some designer set the cosmos in order but does not intervene, answer prayers, or reveal itself. Hitchens treats this as a separate, weaker claim that Turek "tap-dances" into theism.
- **Atheism** — As Hitchens defines it, the position that no argument advanced for God's existence has survived rebuttal; not the dogmatic claim that no God *could* exist.
- **Anti-theism** — Hitchens's stronger self-description: he is not merely unconvinced but *relieved* God does not exist, because such a God would be a tyranny he would oppose. Turek seizes on this in closing ("there is no God and I hate him").
- **Agnosticism** — Suspension of judgment about God's existence; Turek repeatedly cites agnostic astronomer Robert Jastrow as a hostile witness for the Big Bang.
- **Materialism** — The view that only matter exists and that mind, reason, and morality reduce to physical processes ("molecules in motion"). Turek attacks it; Hitchens accepts being a "primate" without conceding the attack.
- **Humanism** — Hitchens's positive source of ethics: solidarity, empathy, and conscience that are innate to the species and require no divine sanction.
- **Christianity** — The specific theistic tradition Turek personally holds but brackets for the debate, offering instead a generic theism; he offers to defend the Resurrection in a "second debate."
- **Naturalism** — The methodological assumption that natural causes suffice to explain the world; Hitchens argues every origin question now has a natural or sufficient explanation.
- **Determinism** — The view (Turek attributes it to Einstein and to Provine's Darwinism) that physical law fixes all events, which Turek says undercuts trust in reasoning.

## Debate Frame

- **Affirmative / Negative** — Turek argues the affirmative (God exists) and speaks first; Hitchens the negative.
- **Burden of proof** — Turek concedes both sides share it ("explain why reality is the way it is"); Hitchens insists the burden falls on the one claiming special knowledge of a creator and his intentions.
- **Opening / Rebuttal / Cross-examination / Closing** — The four phases structuring the event: 20-minute openings, 5-minute rebuttals, direct cross-questioning, audience Q&A, then closings.
- **Moderator** — Dean Timothy Hulley of the VCU Honors College, who keeps time and relays audience questions from cards.
- **United Secular Alliance** — The VCU student group (introduced by president Roy Roberts) that hosted the event.

## The Arguments

- **Cosmological argument** — From the beginning of the universe: if it began, it had a cause/"beginner." Turek's first argument, evidenced by SURGE.
- **Teleological / design argument** — That order and apparent purpose imply a designer; contains both fine-tuning and the design-of-life sub-arguments.
- **Argument from fine-tuning** — That physical constants sit on a "razor's edge" permitting life (gravity to one part in 10^40, the expansion rate, axial tilt, Jupiter as a "cosmic vacuum cleaner"), implying a tuner.
- **Moral argument** — That objective moral values exist and require a moral lawgiver beyond humanity.
- **Ontological argument** — Aquinas/Anselm-style move that to conceive of something is for it to be "real in the mind"; Hitchens opens by accepting then parodying it via limbo and hell.
- **Argument from reason** — That if thoughts are only chemical reactions, we have no grounds to trust reasoning (incl. "Darwin's doubt").
- **Argument from mathematics** — That the rational, mathematical structure of the universe comes from mind, not matter.
- **Argument from free will** — That materialism (per Provine) denies real freedom, yet we evidently have it.
- **Argument from consciousness** — That materialism cannot explain why some carbon-based matter is conscious (Dennett's "illusion").
- **Argument from poor design** — Hitchens's counter: wastefulness, extinction (99.8% of species), the Andromeda collision course, and heat death suggest capricious or absent design.
- **Problem of evil** — Implicit in Hitchens's "created sick, commanded to be well"; suffering and original sin count against a good designer.
- **Choice argument** — Turek's claim that moving from non-existence to existence requires a choice, and only personal beings choose.
- **Probability argument** — Turek's framing in cross-examination: he is not *proving* God but arguing God is the more *probable* explanation across the cumulative evidence.

## Cosmology — the SURGE Evidence

- **Big Bang** — The accepted origin of the universe; Turek "believes in the Big Bang; [he] just thinks [he] knows who banged it."
- **Beginning / Beginner** — The inference chain: a beginning implies a beginner.
- **SURGE** — Turek's acronym for cosmological evidence: **S**econd law, **U**niverse expanding, **R**adiation echo, **G**alaxy seeds, **E**instein's relativity.
- **Second law of thermodynamics / Entropy** — Entropy increases and ordered things tend toward disorder; the universe runs toward heat death, so it cannot be past-eternal.
- **Heat death** — The cold, maximally disordered far-future state; both debaters invoke it (Hitchens for poor design and the "implosion" of the cosmos).
- **Expansion** — Hubble's 1929 discovery that the universe expands; reversed, it collapses to a point.
- **Radiation echo / cosmic afterglow** — The remnant heat of the Big Bang, found by Penzias and Wilson at Bell Labs.
- **COBE** — The 1989 satellite that measured the predicted one-part-in-100,000 temperature variations in the afterglow (George Smoot's result).
- **Galaxy** — The temperature variations seeded the galaxies, "so we could ultimately be here."
- **General relativity** — Einstein's theory making space, time, and matter co-relative, so they originate together.
- **Space-time-matter** — The triad that, per relativity, came into existence together "out of nothing."
- **Singularity** — The point to which reversed expansion mathematically collapses; Turek equates this with "nothing."
- **Red shift / red-light shift** — Hubble's evidence of recession; Krauss's update that the *rate* is increasing.
- **Event horizon / black hole** — Hawking's "lip of the black hole"; Hitchens uses it to show our God-talk is "the vocabulary of our infancy."
- **Andromeda** — The galaxy on a "direct collision course" with ours — Hitchens's exhibit for poor/absent design.
- **Gravitational force / axial tilt** — Two of Turek's fine-tuning constants (gravity to one part in 10^40; Earth's tilt "just right").
- **Nothing** — Defined (via Aristotle) as "what rocks dream about" — no energy, no vacuum, no swirling points.
- **Eternal** — The rejected alternative: an eternal universe would already have reached heat death, so it must have begun.

## Design & Information

- **Specified complexity** — The information content in living systems that Turek says only minds produce.
- **Information** — The DNA "message"; Turek's premise: "information only comes from minds."
- **DNA** — The molecular message carrier; likened to a thousand sets of Encyclopedia Britannica (citing Dawkins).
- **Irreducible complexity** — Raised in audience Q&A about whether such structures could form by chance.
- **Panspermia** — The Crick/Hoyle proposal that life was seeded from space; Turek calls it a backhanded admission against natural origin (it just "puts the question off one more step").
- **Primordial soup** — The hypothesized origin-of-life medium; Wickramasinghe calls belief in it "an article of faith."
- **Natural selection / random mutation** — Darwin's mechanism; Hitchens's sufficient explanation requiring no prime mover ("nothing works without that assumption, everything works with it").
- **Macro-evolution** — Large-scale evolutionary change Turek questions for the *origin* of first life.
- **Watchmaker** — Paley's image: a found watch implies a maker; design implies a designer.
- **Razor's edge** — Turek's metaphor for fine-tuned constants (e.g., gravity to one part in 10^40).
- **Designer / purpose / optimal design** — In closing, Turek argues design need not be *optimal*; you can't call it suboptimal without knowing the designer's purpose.
- **Panda's thumb** — Gould's example of suboptimal design; Turek answers that "maybe all the panda was supposed to do is strip bamboo."

## Moral & Epistemic Concepts

- **Objective moral values** — Mind-independent moral facts Turek says require God; Hitchens grants they feel real but denies they need a deity.
- **Moral lawgiver** — The "prescriber" Turek infers from the "prescription" of objective morality.
- **Standard beyond humanity** — Turek's requirement for grounding right and wrong: "the carbon atom has no moral authority over you."
- **Prescription / prescriber** — Turek's analogy: a prescription implies a prescriber, so objective moral law implies a lawgiver.
- **Is–ought problem** — Hume's point that one cannot derive an *ought* from an *is*; Hitchens grants it and turns it against deriving morality from biology *or* from divine fiat.
- **Empathy** — A biologically grounded human emotion an audience member offers as a moral basis; Turek replies that biology can't make empathy *right*.
- **Conscience** — The inner moral sense; Hitchens traces it through Socrates, Adam Smith, and C. S. Lewis without needing the divine ("why do people behave well when nobody's looking?").
- **Inner daemon** — Socrates's inner critical voice (at his trial), Hitchens's model of conscience.
- **Internal witness** — Adam Smith's term (Theory of Moral Sentiments) for the same.
- **Divine permission / divine command** — The belief that God authorizes action; Hitchens argues the worst atrocities flow precisely from feeling divinely permitted.
- **Moral autonomy** — The intellectual and moral freedom Hitchens says the "celestial dictatorship" is the oldest enemy of.
- **Enlightenment values** — The post-religious values Turek says Hitchens wants everyone to adopt; Turek calls it "the dictatorship of Christopher Hitchens."
- **Solipsism** — In closing, Hitchens names religion's appeal to our self-regard — wanting to be "objects of a divine plan" — and likens it to a quick peek at one's horoscope.

## Theological Concepts

- **Creator** — The being Turek's arguments converge on: spaceless, timeless, immaterial, personal, powerful, intelligent, moral.
- **Spaceless / timeless / immaterial / personal / powerful / intelligent / moral** — The seven attributes Turek derives from the three main arguments.
- **Miracle** — Per Hume, a *suspension* of the natural order; Hitchens argues you can't call the Big Bang (the order's origin) a suspension, only a singularity.
- **Parthenogenesis / virgin birth** — Hitchens grants one could in principle track a pregnancy with "no male intervention," but says it would still not prove divine paternity or true teachings.
- **Resurrection** — A miraculous claim Hitchens says a design argument can never reach; he notes it was "commonplace" in the gospels (Lazarus, the daughter of Jairus, the opened graves).
- **Vicarious redemption** — Salvation by another's suffering; Hitchens's central moral objection: one may pay a debt or even serve a sentence, but cannot assume another's *responsibility*.
- **Scapegoating / human sacrifice** — The "primitive, barbaric" mechanism Hitchens says Christianity plagiarized from Judaism and Islam from both.
- **Atonement** — Turek's reply that Christ's sacrifice is "retroactive to everybody who lived before him," so God "always had a witness."
- **Original sin / the Fall** — Turek's "we were well in the beginning but then we messed up"; Hitchens parodies it as "created sick, commanded to be well."
- **Good Samaritan** — Hitchens's parable: the Samaritan helped "without a religious instruction," and could not have been a Christian since the story precedes Christianity — so morality is "written on your heart."
- **Parable of the talents** — Turek's counter that Jesus did *not* simply preach "take no thought for tomorrow."
- **Limbo** — The (now-abolished) Catholic destination of unbaptized infants; Hitchens's lead example that a thing can be "real to believers" yet abolished.
- **Hell / Heaven** — "Real in the minds" of believers but, Hitchens argues, abolishable mind-forged manacles; he mocks the "new Heaven and new earth" promise.
- **Rapture / Armageddon / apocalypse / eschatology / cult of death** — The end-times yearning Hitchens calls religion's wish for the world to end ("they cannot wait for death and destruction").
- **Mind-forged manacles** — Blake's phrase Hitchens uses for self-imposed religious tyrannies.
- **Celestial dictatorship / totalitarian / theocracy / messianic / master–slave** — Hitchens's framing of theism as submission to an unaccountable supervisor who can "convict us of thought crime"; his post-9/11 cause is opposing theocracy.

## Religion, Ethics & the Body (cross-examination)

- **Abortion / termination of pregnancy** — Turek presses Hitchens on a passage in *God Is Not Great* admitting the unborn child is human yet allowing termination; Hitchens answers that nature, not God, is the "greater abortion provider."
- **Contraception** — Hitchens calls it nonsensical and immoral for the Church to equate contraception with abortion (citing Buckley and Clare Boothe Luce).
- **Ectopic pregnancy** — Hitchens's example of a "no-starter" pregnancy that threatens the mother — a case he says humanism can decide without superstition.
- **Fetus / unborn child / embryology** — Hitchens says he treats the unborn as a "candidate member of society," a view he says embryology's findings of early viability underline.
- **Genital mutilation / suicide bombing / circumcision** — Hitchens's exhibits of wickedness "entirely" or characteristically religious; the deist "knows" what to cut and when.
- **Amalekites (genocide)** — Hitchens's Old Testament exhibit: God's injunction to Moses that too many children were spared — a moral atrocity, not equivalent to a hard medical decision.

## Method & Meta-Reasoning

- **Occam's razor** — Dispose of needless assumptions; Hitchens applies it to remove the "prime mover."
- **Prime mover** — The first-cause designer Hitchens says the analysis ultimately does not need.
- **Hume on miracles** — The maxim that it is always more probable you are mistaken than that nature was suspended in your favor; the heart of the cross-examination clash. Turek replies Hume's "regular beats rare" premise would also deny the Big Bang, one's own birth, and any singular event.
- **Suspension of the natural order** — Hume's definition of a miracle, which Hitchens says cannot apply to the origin of the natural order itself.
- **Non-overlapping magisteria** — Gould's thesis (cited by Hitchens) that science and religion address separate domains; Hitchens thinks they are in many ways irreconcilable.
- **Borrowing from theism / "sit in God's lap to slap his face"** — Turek's charge that Hitchens uses objective morality, reason, logic, mathematics, and the very universe that only theism can ground.
