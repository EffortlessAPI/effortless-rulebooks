# Narrative — The Debate as a Structured Domain

This narrative describes the debate the way the rulebook models it: as **debaters** advancing **arguments**, each built from **premises** and **evidence**, invoking **thinkers** and **quotations**, making **claims** that touch **concepts**, and drawing **rebuttals** from the other side.

Two **debaters** meet under the **United Secular Alliance** at VCU to settle the **affirmative/negative** question *does God exist?* Frank **Turek** takes the affirmative for **theism**; Christopher **Hitchens** takes the negative for **atheism** and, more strongly, **anti-theism**. A **moderator** structures the event into **opening**, **rebuttal**, **cross-examination**, and **closing**, and both accept a shared **burden of proof** to explain reality.

Turek advances three primary **arguments** and four supporting ones. The **cosmological argument** runs from the **Big Bang**: a **beginning** implies a **beginner**. Its **evidence** is the **SURGE** acronym — the **second law of thermodynamics** (which forbids an **eternal** universe headed for **heat death**), the **expansion** Hubble found via **red shift**, the **radiation echo** Penzias and Wilson detected and **COBE** measured, the **galaxy** seeds, and **general relativity**, which makes **space-time-matter** originate together from **nothing** (Aristotle's "what rocks dream about") at a **singularity**. The **teleological / design argument** splits into **fine-tuning** (constants on a **razor's edge**, e.g. gravity to one part in 10^40) and the design of life: **DNA** as **information**, **specified complexity** that "only comes from minds," with **panspermia** and the **primordial soup** as failed natural origins, and the **watchmaker** as the controlling image. The **moral argument** holds that **objective moral values** require a **standard beyond humanity** — a **moral lawgiver**, since a **prescription** needs a **prescriber**. The four supports are the **argument from reason**, the **argument from mathematics**, the **argument from free will**, and the **argument from consciousness**, each attacking **materialism**: chemicals react, they do not reason. From these Turek derives a **creator** that is **spaceless**, **timeless**, **immaterial**, **personal**, **powerful**, **intelligent**, and **moral**.

Hitchens answers from a different frame. He opens on **ontology**: **limbo** and **hell** are "real to believers" — **mind-forged manacles** — yet abolishable, like the **celestial dictatorship** he calls a **totalitarian**, **master–slave** tyranny. He distinguishes **deism** (Paley's order-implies-a-**designer**, the position of **Jefferson**) from **theism**, charging Turek with tap-dancing from one to the other; even granting a **designer**, the theist still owes the path to **answered prayer**, the **virgin birth**, **resurrection**, and **vicarious redemption** by **human sacrifice** and **scapegoating**. On morality he poses two challenges — name a moral act only a believer could do (none), and a wicked one only a believer would do (**suicide bombing**, **genital mutilation**) — and locates atrocity in **divine permission**. He invokes **Occam's razor** and **natural selection** to remove the prime mover, **Hume on miracles** (a **suspension of the natural order** is always less likely than error), and the **is–ought problem** to block deriving morality from biology *or* divine fiat, grounding ethics instead in **empathy**, **conscience**, the **inner daemon** of **Socrates**, and the **internal witness** of **Adam Smith**. His closing names religion's **eschatology** — the **rapture**, **Armageddon**, **apocalypse**, a **cult of death** — as the proof that "religion poisons everything."

The model treats each side's moves as rows: a **claim** is made by a debater, may **support an argument**, touches a **concept**, and can draw a **rebuttal** from the opponent. Turek's signature counter-charge — that Hitchens must **borrow from theism**, "**sit in God's lap to slap his face**" — is itself a claim against Hitchens's claims about **objective moral values**. The derived layer then counts premises per argument, rebuttals per claim, and rolls those up to mark which **arguments** ended the night **contested**.
